01

Understanding GameObjects

Learn the foundation of everything in Unity

A GameObject in Unity is like an empty container. It has properties like position, rotation, and scale, but it's invisible until we add components to it. Think of it as an empty box that can hold anything you want.
VIDEO TRANSCRIPT

"What is actually a GameObject? An empty GameObject is an invisible container with properties like position, rotation, and scale."

Fig 1: What is GameObject
PRO TIP

Think of GameObjects as empty boxes. They need components (like SpriteRenderer) to become visible or functional in your game.

02

Creating an Empty GameObject

Two methods to create your first object

There are two ways to create an empty GameObject in Unity. You can either right-click in the Hierarchy panel, or use the top menu bar. Both methods achieve the same result.
VIDEO TRANSCRIPT

"We're going to right-click on the Hierarchy panel and create an empty GameObject. You have two ways: right-click → Create Empty, or GameObject menu → Create Empty."

Fig 2a: Creating GameObject via right-click in Hierarchy
Fig 2b: Creating GameObject via top menu bar
03

Naming and Importing Assets

Organize your project properly

After creating the GameObject, rename it to "Bird" for clarity. Then import your bird image asset into Unity. Good naming conventions and organization are essential for larger projects.
VIDEO TRANSCRIPT

"I'm going to name my GameObject 'Bird'. I saved the bird image on my computer. Go to Assets → Import New Asset, select the bird image, and import it."

04

Adding SpriteRenderer

Make your GameObject visible

The SpriteRenderer component is what makes your GameObject visible on screen. Without it, your bird would remain invisible even if you have the sprite ready.
VIDEO TRANSCRIPT

"We can add many components to the GameObject. SpriteRenderer is going to make it visible. Click Add Component, look for Rendering, then Sprite Renderer."

Fig 4: Adding the SpriteRenderer component
PRO TIP

Without a SpriteRenderer, your GameObject remains invisible even if it has a sprite assigned. The component is essential for 2D games!

05

Assigning the Sprite

Connect your image to the renderer

Now drag your bird image from the Project panel to the Sprite field in the SpriteRenderer component. This connects your visual asset to the component that renders it.
VIDEO TRANSCRIPT

"Click on the bird in the Project panel and drag it to the Sprite field. Once I drag it, the bird appears in my scene."

Fig 5: Dragging and assigning the sprite to the renderer
06

Viewing in Game View

Check your results and set resolution

Switch to Game view to see how your bird will actually appear when the game runs. You can also adjust the screen resolution to match your target platform.
VIDEO TRANSCRIPT

"If I click on Game, we have here the game view with our bird. I can control the resolution by clicking 'Free Aspect' and choosing 1920 by 1080."

Fig 6a: Bird appearing in Game view
Fig 6b: Setting game resolution to 1920x1080
07

Adjusting Size and Scale

Perfect your bird's appearance

Finally, adjust the Transform scale to make the bird the right size for your game. The initial sprite might be too large or too small for your intended gameplay.
VIDEO TRANSCRIPT

"The next step is to control the size or scale of our bird because it appears really big on the screen."

Fig 7: Adjusting the bird's scale in Transform component

Complete Process Summary

  1. Create empty GameObject (right-click in Hierarchy)
  2. Rename it to "Bird" for clarity
  3. Import bird image (Assets → Import New Asset)
  4. Add SpriteRenderer component
  5. Drag bird image to Sprite field
  6. View in Game tab, set resolution
  7. Adjust Transform scale as needed
