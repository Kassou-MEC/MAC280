02

Adding Rigidbody2D Component

Turn your bird into a physics object

The Rigidbody2D component transforms our bird into a physics object with gravity. We use "2D" because we're making a 2D game. Make sure you're on the Scene panel (not Game), select the bird, then go to the Inspector to add the component.
Video Transcript

"Make sure you are on the scene panel not the game panel... highlight the bird, come to the inspector side... click add component and then we're going to look for Rigidbody... I'm going to choose Rigidbody 2D, this is the one responsible for physics in 2D games."

Fig 2: Adding Rigidbody2D component to the bird
Important

If you don't see Rigidbody in the dropdown, just type the first letter "R" in the search bar to find it quickly.

03

Understanding Rigidbody2D Properties

Gravity Scale, Mass, and Damping explained

Once Rigidbody2D is added, your bird becomes affected by gravity. The component has several important properties that control how physics affects your object.
Video Transcript

"We can increase or decrease the gravity scale but by default it is set to one and the mass, the weight of our bird is also set to one. For linear damping and angular damping I would explain this in future lectures."

Gravity Scale (Default: 1) Controls how strongly gravity affects the object. Higher = falls faster.
Mass (Default: 1) The weight of your object. Affects collision interactions.
Linear & Angular Damping Controls how quickly movement and rotation slow down over time.
Fig 3: Rigidbody2D properties in the Inspector

Now let's test our physics! When you play the game, gravity will be applied to the bird and it will fall down. You can stop the game to return to the initial position.

06

Adding Circle Collider 2D

Enable collision detection

After adding Rigidbody2D, we need the bird to interact with other objects. The Circle Collider 2D component allows collision detection. We use a circle because our bird has a circular shape.
Video Transcript

"After adding the component Rigidbody 2D to our bird we want also the bird to be able to interact with other objects. So let's add another component... the component is called Circle Collider. Why specifically Circle Collider? Because the shape of our bird is circular."

Fig 6: Adding Circle Collider 2D component
Collider Types

Unity offers different collider shapes: Circle, Box, Polygon, Edge, and Capsule. Choose based on your sprite's shape!

07

Adjusting the Collider Radius

Fine-tune the collision boundary

The collider should be slightly smaller than the bird sprite for better collision detection. Adjust the radius in the Inspector, or use the Scale tool to resize the bird to fit inside the collider.
Video Transcript

"The best way to make the circle collider effective is to make it a little bit smaller than our bird. I'm going to come to the inspector side... control the radius of our collider... you see here we have one arrow on the left side, one arrow on the right side, and then on the scene panel the circle becomes smaller."

Fig 7: Adjusting the Circle Collider radius
08

Circle Collider Roles & Features

Understanding what colliders do

The Circle Collider 2D serves multiple purposes in your game. Understanding these roles helps you build better physics interactions.
Collision Detection Detects collisions with other 2D objects using circular shape - ideal for round objects.
Physics Interaction Enables realistic physical interactions like bouncing and sliding based on circular boundary.
Trigger Functionality Can act as trigger to detect overlaps without physical collision.
Adjustable Radius Allows fine-tuning of collision area to match visual representation.
Fig 8: Understanding collider functionality
